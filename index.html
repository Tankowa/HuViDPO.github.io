DPVDM: Enhancing Video Generation through Direct Preference Optimization for Human-Centric Alignment

Lifan Jiang, Jiahui Zhang, Xiaotong Guan, Boxi Wu*
State Key Lab of CAD&CG, Zhejiang University
CVPR 2025
*Corresponding Author
Paper Code arXiv

Abstract

With the rapid development of AI-generated content, text-to-image (T2I) technology based on diffusion models has made significant progress, driving innovations in text-to-video (T2V) generation methods. However, existing T2V generation methods often struggle to meet human visual preferences due to the lack of high-quality, large-scale training data, and they demand substantial computational resources. To address these challenges, we propose the DPVDM method, which enhances human-centric alignment in T2V tasks through Direct Preference Optimization (DPO). Our approach fine-tunes the Video Diffusion Model (VDM) by constructing small-scale human preference datasets for each action category, thereby aligning VDM with human preferences and improving the subjective quality of generated videos. To reduce training costs, we use a small video dataset to learn a common motion pattern, applying Low-Rank Adaptation(LORA) to fine-tune the model, allowing it to complete the fine-tuning in one day on a single RTX4090 GPU. Subsequently, to address the issues of content overfitting and lack of flexibility, and to ensure temporal and spatial consistency, we optimized the spatio-temporal attention layers, further enhancing the coherence and visual appeal of the generated videos, ensuring smooth transitions between frames.

🔥 Learn T2V Generation with Only 8~16 Videos 🔥

Framework of the proposed LAMP. Our LAMP learns a motion pattern from a small video set and generates videos with the learned motion, which achieves the trade-off between training burden and generation freedom. We transfer text-to-video generation to the first-frame generation and subsequent-frame prediction, i.e., decoupling a video's contents and motions. During the training stage, We add noise and compute loss functions on all but the first frame. Moreover, only the parameters of newly added layers and the query linear layers of self-attention blocks are updated when tuning. During the inference stage, we use a T2I model to generate the first frame. The tuned model only works on denoising the latent features of subsequent frames with the guidance of user prompts.
